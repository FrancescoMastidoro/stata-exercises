## Background
The `nlsw88` dataset contains 1988 survey data on American women aged 34–46, covering wages, employment, education, and demographics from the National Longitudinal Survey. Knowing how to quickly inspect and summarize a dataset is the foundation of any empirical workflow — before running a single regression, you need to understand what variables you have, how they are coded, and where the data might have gaps.

## Your tasks

1. **Load and inspect** — Load the dataset and use `describe` to get an overview of all variables. How many observations and variables does the dataset have? What is the storage type of the variable `wage`?

2. **Summarize continuous variables** — Use `summarize` to produce basic statistics for `wage`, `age`, and `ttl_exp` (total work experience). Note the mean, minimum, and maximum for each.

3. **Detailed summary** — Re-run `summarize` on `wage` alone, this time adding the `detail` option. What are the 25th and 75th percentiles? Does the gap between the median and the mean suggest anything about the shape of the wage distribution?

4. **Tabulate a categorical variable** — Use `tabulate` on the variable `industry`. Then use `tabulate` on `industry` and `union` together (two-way tabulation) to see how union membership is distributed across industries. Which industry has the highest number of union members?

5. *(Bonus)* **Conditional summary** — Use `summarize wage if union == 1` and `summarize wage if union == 0` separately. How large is the raw wage gap between union and non-union workers? Think about what confounders might make this simple comparison misleading.
