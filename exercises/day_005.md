## Background
The `lifeexp` dataset contains country-level data on life expectancy, GNP per capita, and related demographic indicators for a cross-section of countries. Knowing how to quickly audit a new dataset's structure, check variable types, and produce basic descriptive statistics is an essential first step before any quantitative analysis — it reveals data quality issues, guides variable choices, and prevents costly misinterpretations downstream.

## Your tasks

1. **Load and inspect** — Load the dataset with `sysuse lifeexp, clear` and run `describe`. How many observations and variables does the dataset have? Identify any variable that is stored as a string and any variable stored as a labeled numeric. Write a comment in your do-file noting what real-world concept each identified variable captures.

2. **Summarize a continuous variable** — Run `summarize` on the variable `lexp` (life expectancy at birth). Record the mean, standard deviation, minimum, and maximum in comments in your do-file. Does the range between the minimum and maximum seem wide or narrow given what you know about global health inequality? Write one sentence of interpretation.

3. **Detailed distribution of life expectancy** — Re-run `summarize lexp` with the `detail` option. Compare the mean to the 50th percentile. Is the distribution roughly symmetric, or does it appear skewed? Write a comment stating the direction of any skew and what it suggests about whether most countries cluster near the top or the bottom of the distribution.

4. **Tabulate a categorical variable and compare group means** — Use `tabulate` on the variable `region` to see how many countries fall into each regional category. Then use `summarize lexp` with an appropriate `if` condition to compute the mean life expectancy separately for at least two of the regions shown in the tabulation. Which region has the higher mean, and how large is the gap in years relative to the overall mean?

5. *(Bonus)* **GNP distribution within regions** — Use `tabulate` to identify the region with the most observations. Then run `summarize gnppc, detail` restricted to that region and retrieve its median GNP per capita and its interquartile range (75th minus 25th percentile). Compare those figures to the overall median and IQR from an unrestricted `summarize gnppc, detail`. Write a comment discussing whether that region is richer or poorer than the global sample and whether income is more or less dispersed within it.
